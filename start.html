Facts about Martin Luther King Jr.

Martin Luther King Jr. was a baptist minister, an American Civil Rights activist, and widely known for his role in the American Civil Rights movement from 1955 until his assasination in 1968.

Born Michael King Jr, Dr. King was the youngest of Michael King Jr. and Alberta Williams King. He was born on January 15, 1929 in Atlanta, Georgia. He helped lead the Montgomery Bus Boycott, when he marched for 381 days straight!

And that was nothing compared to what he had to face later on in life. King later got arrested 29 times, for simple things such as driving at 30 miles per hour in a 25 miles per hour zone.
